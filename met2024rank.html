How does this work? >

The code works on the principle of linear interpolation:

In MET 2020, board marks were implemented with a 60-40 weightageâ€”60% being MET and 40% board marks. Data compiled from various sources (YouTube, Reddit, Quora, and direct student input) is used to compute band percentages with the following formula:

\( \left( \frac{M}{800} \right) \times 0.6 + (B \times 0.4) \)

Where M stands for your MET mark out of 800, and B is your board percentage. After calculating band marks, these scores are plotted and compared against their ranks.

Using a mathematical concept called Linear Interpolation, we can estimate the ranks for different band scores. Note that:

  • In areas with large gaps in data, the predictions may be less accurate.
  • MET 2020 was conducted online, leading to possible rank inflation due to cheating.
  • Other factors might also affect the accuracy of predictions.

However, this remains the best method to predict this year's rank.

Why use the 1 band system instead of 0.5?

ChronocRafter (credit to him) discovered that the data best matched when using the 1 band score rather than 0.5 or a 60-40 average. With other methods, you might see better band scores correlating with worse ranks. For a detailed look at the data, please review the table below:
